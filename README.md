# Insurance Data Analysis using Power BI

## üß©Problem Statement
Insurance companies deal with large volumes of policy, premium, and claim data, but turning this data into meaningful insights is often challenging. Without a clear analytical view, it becomes difficult to understand which policy types generate the most revenue, how claims are distributed across customer segments, and where potential risk or inefficiencies exist in the claims process.

This project aims to analyze insurance data stored in a MySQL database and present it through a structured, multi-page Power BI report. The goal is to provide a clear overview of premium collection, coverage exposure, claim status, and customer demographics, enabling better visibility into claim behavior, policy performance, and operational decision-making.

&nbsp;

## üìãObjectives
* Provide an overall view of key insurance metrics such as total premium amount, coverage amount, and claim amount to understand the scale of business operations.

* Analyze the distribution of insurance claims across different claim statuses (settled, rejected, pending) to assess claims processing effectiveness.

* Compare premium contributions across policy types (Travel, Health, Auto, Life, Home) to identify the most revenue-generating insurance products.

+ Examine claim amounts across age groups to understand how claim behavior varies among different customer segments.

* Evaluate the proportion of active and inactive policies to identify potential retention risks and portfolio health.

## üí° Key Insights

* Travel and Health insurance policies generate the highest premium amounts, making them the most significant contributors to overall revenue.

* A considerable portion of claims remains either pending or rejected, indicating potential inefficiencies or stricter evaluation within the claims process.

* Middle-aged and adult customers account for the highest claim amounts, suggesting higher insurance utilization within these age groups.

* Inactive policies form a noticeable share of the portfolio, highlighting opportunities for customer re-engagement and retention strategies.

* Premium and claim amounts vary significantly by policy type, showing that risk exposure is not evenly distributed across insurance products.
